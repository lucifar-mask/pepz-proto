MUSCLE RECOVERY

Peptide-based recovery protocols work by accelerating the body's own repair signaling rather than masking pain. BPC-157 and TB-500 promote angiogenesis (new blood vessel growth) into damaged tendons, ligaments, and gut tissue, areas that are naturally slow to heal due to poor blood supply. This translates to faster tissue regeneration after strains, tears, or surgery, while also calming systemic inflammation and supporting gut lining integrity, which is often compromised by intense training. The result is a shorter window between injury and return to full output: rehab that used to take months can compress into weeks, letting athletes train harder, recover cleaner, and stay consistent without the setbacks that chronic micro-injuries usually cause.

LONGEVITY

LONGEVITY

Longevity-focused peptides work upstream of the visible signs of aging, targeting the cellular machinery that determines how well — and how long — the body keeps repairing itself. Compounds like NAD+ and Epitalon restore mitochondrial energy production and support circadian rhythm, helping cells generate the fuel they need for repair while deepening the restorative sleep where most regeneration happens. Others reinforce immune resilience and cellular turnover, addressing the slow breakdown in repair efficiency that drives age-related decline rather than just its symptoms. Used together, these protocols aren't about slowing a clock — they're about restoring the cellular conditions for a longer, more resilient healthspan.

BEAUTY

BEAUTY

Beauty-focused peptides work by replacing signals the skin naturally stops producing with age, rather than masking the surface effects of that decline. GHK-Cu, a copper-binding peptide the body produces abundantly in youth and far less by the thirties, rebuilds the dermal matrix from below — stimulating collagen, elastin, and glycosaminoglycan synthesis to restore thickness and elasticity while accelerating repair of photodamage and supporting denser hair growth at the follicle. Rather than sitting on top of the skin like a topical treatment, these peptides work systemically and topically to repair the underlying structure, producing results that compound over weeks rather than fading the moment you stop using them.

FITNESS

FITNESS

Fitness-focused peptides work by amplifying signals the body already uses to build and maintain lean tissue, rather than introducing something foreign to it. Growth-hormone secretagogues like CJC-1295 and Ipamorelin nudge the pituitary to pulse natural GH overnight, supporting lean muscle mass and shortening recovery windows between sessions, while mitochondrial peptides like MOTS-C boost exercise capacity and metabolic efficiency at the cellular level. The effect compounds across training, recovery, and cognition — deeper sleep, leaner body composition, faster bounce-back from hard sessions, and a sharper edge for athletes, lifters, and anyone pushing to optimize physical output.

THE EVIDENCE

Hover a card to see the actual research behind each compound.

BPC-157

Muscle Recovery

Preclinical

Extensively studied in animal models for tissue and gut-lining repair; no completed human clinical trials have been published.

Promising in animal research — human evidence doesn't exist yet, so expectations should stay realistic.

FDA Category 2 Bulk Drug Substances list (fda.gov), 2023

TB-500

Muscle Recovery

Preclinical

Animal and cell studies suggest pro-healing, anti-inflammatory activity; no completed human safety or efficacy trials exist.

Backed by lab and animal data, not yet by human trials.

FDA Category 2 Bulk Drug Substances list (fda.gov); ClinicalTrials.gov

Sermorelin

Longevity

FDA-Approved(historical / different indication)

Originally FDA-approved as Geref (1997) for diagnosing and treating growth hormone deficiency in children; the branded product was discontinued in 2008 for commercial, not safety, reasons.

A once FDA-approved GH therapy — today's adult longevity use is an off-label, compounded application.

FDA Federal Register determination, GEREF (sermorelin acetate), 2013

NAD+

Longevity

Preclinical

No large randomized controlled human trials have established IV NAD+ therapy's anti-aging benefits; evidence so far is mechanistic and comes mainly from animal studies of NAD+ metabolism.

Early-stage research on a well-understood cellular pathway — human proof for anti-aging claims isn't there yet.

Clinical Evidence for Targeting NAD Therapeutically, PMC, 2020

PT-141

Longevity

FDA-Approved

FDA-approved as Vyleesi (2019) based on two Phase 3 RECONNECT trials, which showed significantly increased sexual desire and satisfying sexual events vs. placebo in premenopausal women with HSDD.

The only compound on this list backed by full FDA approval and Phase 3 trial data.

FDA-approved prescribing information, VYLEESI (bremelanotide), Initial U.S. Approval 2019

CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin

Fitness

Clinical Trial

A 2006 study in the Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism found CJC-1295 produced sustained, dose-dependent increases in growth hormone and IGF-1 in healthy adults over several weeks.

Human data confirms the GH/IGF-1 effect; larger trials on muscle or fat outcomes are still lacking.

Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism, 2006

Tesamorelin

Fitness

FDA-Approved(different indication)

FDA-approved as Egrifta/Egrifta WR based on two trials in 816 HIV-positive adults, showing significant reduction in visceral abdominal fat via CT scan vs. placebo.

Real FDA approval and real trial data — though for HIV-related fat loss, not general fitness; use here is off-label.

FDA-approved EGRIFTA prescribing information, accessdata.fda.gov

MOTS-C

Fitness

Preclinical

Discovered in 2015 as a mitochondria-derived "exercise mimetic" peptide; research remains mostly in animal and cell models, with human trials for metabolic conditions only just beginning.

One of the newest peptides in research — a promising mechanism, but human evidence is still in its infancy.

MOTS-c: A promising mitochondrial-derived peptide for therapeutic exploitation, PMC, 2023

GHK-Cu

Beauty

Clinical Trial

A 12-week clinical trial in women with photoaged skin measured up to a 28% increase in collagen density with daily topical use.

One of the better-studied peptides on this list, with real human skin-trial data behind it.

Pickart & Margolina, Biomolecules, 2018 (systematic review of GHK-Cu clinical trials)
